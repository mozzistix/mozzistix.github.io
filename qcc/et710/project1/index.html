Antarctica

Antarctica is the least-populated continent on Earth. It is a polar region around Earth's South Pole and is opposite the Arctic region around the North Pole.

Here are some fun facts!

  1. Antarctica contains about 90% of the world's ice and about 70% of the world's fresh water.
  2. With temperatures as low as -128 F, Antarctica is the coldest place on Earth.
  3. Antarctica is the 5th largest continent and is double the size of Australia. However, it doesn't have a capital city or an official language, and no country controls it.
  4. Antarctica is a desert. It only gets about 2 inches of snow each year. The snow there is old, and it never melts.
  5. The male emperor penguin is the only warm-blooded animal that stays in Antarctica through the winter.
  6. Adele penguins live in Antarctica. They weight just 10 pounds and can dive up to 600 feet under water.
  7. Even though krill are about the size of small paper clip, krill swarms in Antarctica can be seen from space.
